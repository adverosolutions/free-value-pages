Inquiry Response Flow

It's 95° on a Saturday. A homeowner's AC stops working. They find Elite HVAC Services online and submit an inquiry. 78% of homeowners hire whoever responds first (Harvard Business Review). They're also contacting 3-5 other HVAC companies.

Phase 1: Qualifying Leads

When It Depends on Your Team

  • Owner or dispatcher reads every reply manually
  • No consistent criteria for what makes a good lead
  • High-intent prospects sit in inbox while low-intent get same attention
  • Time wasted on tire kickers and out-of-area inquiries
  • Gut-check decisions made when already busy on a job
  • Emergency vs routine jobs treated the same way

When It Runs on Its Own

  • Replies sorted by intent automatically
  • High-intent leads surface immediately
  • Low-intent filtered into slower nurture sequence
  • Out-of-area or unqualified leads identified upfront
  • Right leads get attention first, regardless of how busy the day is
  • Emergency jobs flagged and prioritized instantly

Phase 2: First Response

When It Depends on Your Team

  • Saturday inquiry → Monday response = Lost to competitor
  • After 5 minutes, conversion drops 80% (MIT study)
  • Tech in attic = 3 hours before anyone replies
  • 10 PM emergency? Waits until morning
  • Average HVAC company response: 42-48 hours
  • 30-40% of leads never get contacted at all

When It Runs on Its Own

  • Response in under 60 seconds, every time
  • Catches them while they're still searching
  • Works while your team is on jobs
  • 2 AM emergency? Already handled
  • 8 touchpoints over 4 days automatically
  • 100% of leads get followed up
