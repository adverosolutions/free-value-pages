Inquiry Response Flow

Storm damage last night. A homeowner wakes up to a leaking roof. They find Peak Performance Roofing and submit an inquiry. They're contacting 5-10 other roofers. After a storm, 70% of jobs go to companies that respond in the first 24 hours.

Phase 1: Qualifying Leads

When It Depends on Your Team

  • Owner or estimator reads every inquiry manually
  • No way to tell full replacement vs small repair upfront
  • Active leaks treated same as general inspections
  • Out-of-service-area leads waste time
  • Can't tell if they're shopping 10 roofers or ready to book
  • Storm surge = 50+ inquiries, no way to prioritize

When It Runs on Its Own

  • Leads sorted by job size and urgency automatically
  • Full replacements flagged and prioritized instantly
  • Active leaks get immediate attention
  • Out-of-area leads filtered before wasting estimator time
  • High-intent homeowners surface first
  • Storm surge handled without missing the best jobs

Phase 2: First Response

When It Depends on Your Team

  • Storm Saturday → Response Monday = Already hired someone else
  • After storms, 10-20x normal inquiries = Can't keep up
  • Crew on a job = 4-6 hours before anyone sees it
  • Insurance deadline approaching? Still waiting
  • Average roofer response: 3-5 days (by then, 85% have booked)
  • 43% of storm leads never get contacted at all

When It Runs on Its Own

  • Response in under 60 seconds, even during storms
  • Handles 100+ inquiries without breaking
  • Works while crews are on roofs
  • Urgent? They know you got their request
  • Persistent follow-up captures busy homeowners
  • Every lead gets 8 touchpoints automatically
